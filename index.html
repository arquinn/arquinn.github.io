Hi there, I'm Andrew Quinn!

I'm a second year Ph.d student working with Jason Flinn at the University of Michigan. My research interests are in the area of operating systems and distributed systems. Specifically, I'm building systems that make complex programming analyses fast, and investigating how these systems enable novel tools.

My first research project, Jetstream, was just accepted to OSDI '16! Jetstream applies cluster computing to parallelize dynamic information flow tracking (DIFT), a tool which helps us understand causality in a computer system. Jetstream scales to at least 128 cores and attains an order of magnitude speedup over sequential DIFT.
